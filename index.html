The Hidden Kingdom of Fungi

It encompasses an estimated 2.2 to 3.8 million species, with only around 120,000 identified so far.

Its importance

Fungi hold crucial importance in various aspects of life. They contribute to ecosystem equilibrium by breaking down organic matter, recycling nutrients, and maintaining soil quality. Through symbiotic relationships, fungi aid plants in nutrient absorption and form resilient partnerships like lichens. Fungi also yield essential medicines, including antibiotics like penicillin.
